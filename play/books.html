The Salmon of Doubt, Douglas Adams.
I was a bit unsure about getting this book. It is made up of over 3,000 documents that were retrieved from Douglas Adam's beloved Macintosh computers - most of which were unpublished and I suspected that Douglas had good reasons for this.. However, so far I don't regret buying it at all, the introduction to the book by Stephen Fry made me feel a bit better about reading these unfinished works, and there have been sections, espeacially the one about a couple of dogs, that have had me laughing hysterically and feel incredibly sad at the same time.

Designing Web Usability, Jakob Nielson.
I'm doing a presentation at the moment about web design. I bought this book as Jakob Nielsen is one of the guru's of web design. I disagree with some of his conclusions (they are a bit out of date), however the book contains a lot of statistical evidence regarding actual web usuage and it is good to have a book to point at in the presentation and say "do it because he says so..."
